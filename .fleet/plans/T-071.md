# Issue #358 / T-071 — canonical plan (architect direction)

**Status:** `fleet:design-unblocked` after the NEEDS-DESIGN escalation on PR
[#423](https://github.com/jakildev/IrredenEngine/pull/423). The original
acceptance read as "delete all the occupancy-grid machinery." The design doc
at `docs/design/screen-space-sun-shadow-map.md` only audited the sun-shadow
consumer; AO and light-volume also pull on the grid. This plan narrows
T-071's scope to the sun-shadow paths (which are what the design doc
actually covered) and routes AO + light-volume migration through dedicated
follow-up tasks.

## What T-071 deletes (scope of the resumed PR)

All of these are sun-shadow scope and gated behind `useScreenSpaceShadow_`
today, OR exist only to feed the legacy DDA / analytic march:

1. **Analytic SDF caster path** —
   - `SunShadowShapeCasterBuffer` named resource +
     `kBufferIndex_SunShadowShapeCasters` constant.
   - `detail::collectShapeCastersForCanvas`, sun-cone culling,
     `analyticShapeShadowHit`.
   - Per-shape SDF helper functions in `c_compute_sun_shadow.glsl` and
     `.metal`.
   - `shapeCasterCount` field of `FrameDataSun` (and the upload of it).
2. **Legacy DDA branch** —
   - The `useScreenSpaceShadow == 0` block in
     `c_compute_sun_shadow.{glsl,metal}` (the 64-step Amanatides-Woo march).
   - `OccupancyEntityBounds` SSBO, `kBufferIndex_OccupancyEntityBounds`
     constant, `detail::occupancyEntityBoundsCount()`,
     `occupancyBoundsCount` field of `FrameDataSun`. Bounds existed only
     for the per-pixel self-skip in the legacy DDA.
3. **Feature-flag plumbing** —
   - `FrameDataSun::useScreenSpaceShadow_` (becomes always-on, then
     deleted).
   - `IRRender::setScreenSpaceShadowsEnabled` API stays for one cycle as a
     no-op stub (so creations don't break) **or** is deleted outright with
     a search-and-replace through `creations/`. Author's call; preference
     is delete-outright since only `shape_debug` and
     `lighting_demo_scene` reference it.
   - `--screen-space-shadow` opt-in flag in `creations/demos/shape_debug/main.cpp`
     and `creations/demos/lighting/common/lighting_demo_scene.hpp` —
     remove. Default becomes screen-space.
4. **SDF-rasterization step in `BUILD_OCCUPANCY_GRID`** — lines 163-281 of
   `engine/prefabs/irreden/render/systems/system_build_occupancy_grid.hpp`
   (the `BuildOccupancyGrid::PopulateSDFs` block). This step rasterizes SDF
   shape entities into the occupancy bitfield **only** so the sun-shadow
   DDA can see them. AO and light-volume only ever needed voxel-pool
   occupancy. Removing this is in-scope for T-071 — it's analytic-caster
   feeder code, not AO/light-volume support.

## What T-071 keeps

These survive until the AO + light-volume follow-ups land:

- `BUILD_OCCUPANCY_GRID` system (voxel-pool population only after the SDF
  step is removed).
- `C_OccupancyGrid` component + `OccupancyGridBuffer` SSBO.
- `kBufferIndex_OccupancyGrid` constant.
- The slot-28 alias (`kBufferIndex_SunShadowDepthMap = kBufferIndex_OccupancyGrid`).
  Both AO and the new sun-shadow bake bind their respective buffer to slot 28
  before dispatch; the alias stays correct.
- `system_compute_voxel_ao.hpp` + `c_compute_voxel_ao.{glsl,metal}` —
  unchanged; still binds `OccupancyGridBuffer` and reads `occupancyGetBit`.
- `system_compute_light_volume.hpp` — unchanged; still reads
  `C_OccupancyGrid` for BFS / LOS.

## Follow-ups (file as new TASKS.md issues)

These are NOT part of T-071 and will be filed as separate issues. Listed
here so the worker resuming T-071 knows the long-range plan and writes
the right CLAUDE.md "phased-out" framing.

### T-091 — AO via screen-space neighbor sampling (issue #428)

Migrate `c_compute_voxel_ao.{glsl,metal}` off the occupancy SSBO. Read
4 face-tangent neighbour pixels in `trixelDistances` instead. Receiver-
vs-neighbour iso-depth comparison along the face-outward direction
gives the same crease-darkening behaviour without the world-space
bitfield.

Open question for the next agent: does the iso-axis-aligned occluder-
occluded-by-receiver case matter visually? Empirically rare for iso
because the visible face IS the closest surface along iso depth.
Validate against existing AO screenshots via `render-debug-loop`;
acceptable diff is bias-only at crease edges.

Drops AO from the occupancy grid. Cost stays O(canvasPixels) with 4
texel reads per pixel.

### T-072 — GPU light-volume propagation (already filed, blocked-by T-071)

See `~/.fleet/plans/issue-359.md`. Architectural choice the design doc
punted on: jump-flood with a small GPU-built voxel-pool-only occupancy
for v1; projective light-space depth maps for omni LOS deferred to a
later unification task. SDF LOS regression (#364) folds into whichever
path wins.

### T-092 — Final occupancy-grid teardown (issue #429)

After both T-091 and T-072 land. Delete:
- `BUILD_OCCUPANCY_GRID` system, `C_OccupancyGrid` component,
  `OccupancyGridBuffer` SSBO + named resource.
- `kBufferIndex_OccupancyGrid` constant; retire the slot-28 alias and
  promote `kBufferIndex_SunShadowDepthMap = 28` as the canonical name.
- The CPU↔GPU `roundHalfUp` parity contract (no longer needed once no
  consumer reads world-space cell coords from the bitfield).
- Phased-out CLAUDE.md sections from T-071.

Trivial PR once consumers are gone — pure deletion.

## Long-range architectural framing (no task yet)

The `trixelDistances` texture is the single source of truth for "what's
the closest surface at iso pixel P, and which face." The pattern T-070
established generalises: any "is there geometry along ray R" or "what's
the nearest occluder in direction D" question becomes a projective
depth-map lookup against a buffer baked from `trixelDistances`. Sun
shadow is the first instance. Spot light LOS would be the second
(perspective shadow map per spot). Omni point-light LOS would be the
third (cube-map flavour). The CPU-built world-space occupancy SSBO is
the wrong shape under this pattern. This framing should drive the
T-072 design discussion — but the v1 jump-flood with a small GPU-built
voxel occupancy is a fine intermediate that doesn't require committing
to projective LOS yet.
