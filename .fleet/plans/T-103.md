## This PR: Hot-reload of Lua system bodies

### Scope

Allow a Lua-defined system's tick body to be replaced in place at runtime, without recreating
the system or migrating any entity data. Component-schema hot-reload is explicitly **out of
scope** here (recorded as a follow-up in `docs/design/lua-driven-ecs.md`).

### Deliverables

- `IRSystem::replaceSystemBody(systemId, newFn)` on the C++ side — rebinds the `sol::function`
  held by the existing dynamic system. No archetype changes, no entity migration, `SystemId`
  unchanged.
- Lua binding: `IRSystem.replaceSystemBody(systemId, newFn)`.
- Documentation in `engine/script/CLAUDE.md` alongside the existing trait-based binding pattern.

### Verification

Smoke test: change a math constant inside a `Regen` tick body in `main.lua`, call
`IRSystem.replaceSystemBody(regenSystemId, newRegenFn)`, observe the next-tick behavior
change without process restart. The system's `SystemId` is unchanged across the swap.
In-flight entities continue using the new body on the next tick with no special handling.
